Packet Tracer - Create Realistic Structured Cabling in the Physical Workspace and Cabling Devices in a Rack

Objectives

In this activity, you will install a patch panel and a wall mount. You will then use these to connect network devices in the office to the equipment in the wiring closet.

Part 1: Install a Patch Panel in the Wiring Closet

Part 2: Attach a Wall Mount in the Office

Part 3: Connect an Additional Wall Mount and Cables

Required Resources

Latest Packet Tracer version

Instructions

Part 1: Install a Patch Panel in the Wiring Closet

Step 1: Install a patch panel in the rack

a. Click the Equipment Cabinet to access a simulated Wiring Closet.

b. To install a patch panel, click Connections > Structured Cabling in the Device-Type Selection Box.

c. In the Device-Specific Selection Box, click Copper Patch Panel.

d. Click a location in the Rack to install the patch panel.

Note: For accurate grading, ensure the patch panel name is Patch Panel0.

Step 2: Connect the Office-SW1 to the patch panel

a. From the Cable Pegboard, select a Copper Straight-Through cable.

b. On Office-SW1, click port GigabitEthernet 1/0/13, then click Jack13 on Patch Panel0.

Note: You can right-click devices, choose Inspect Front, and zoom for easier port selection.

c. Use the table to finish connections between Patch Panel0 and Office-SW1.

Office-SW1 Patch Panel0
G1/0/13Jack13
G1/0/14Jack14
G1/0/15Jack15
G1/0/16Jack16

d. (Optional) Color code rack cables: right-click cable > Color Cable.

e. Organize cables: right-click rack white space > Manage All Cables on Rack.

f. Click Back level (Alt+Left) to return to the Office.

Part 2: Attach a Wall Mount in the Office

Step 1: Install a wall mount

a. Click Connections > Structured Cabling.

b. Select Copper Wall Mount.

c. Click a location on the wall next to the Equipment Cabinet.

Note: Wall mount name must be Wall Mount0.

d. Select Connections > Copper Straight-Through.

e. Connect Wall Mount0 PunchDown1 to Patch Panel0 Punchdown13 (Rack path).

f. Repeat for remaining punchdowns:

Wall Mount0 (Next to Equipment Cabinet) Patch Panel0
Punchdown1Punchdown13
Punchdown2Punchdown14
Punchdown3Punchdown15
Punchdown4Punchdown16

g. Connect Office-Admin PC and Printer0 to any available wall mount jacks with Copper Straight-Through cables. DHCP (Office-Server) will assign addresses.

h. Verify connectivity: Office-Admin > Desktop > Web Browser and browse to office.srv (may take a minute; click Go to refresh).

Step 2: Organize the cables

In Physical mode you can route cables along walls.

a. Right-click a cable > Create BendPoint.

b. Drag bend points to walls/floor until cable is neat.

c. Repeat as needed.
